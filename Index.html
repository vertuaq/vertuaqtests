Introduction to E-Commerce

E-commerce refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. It has revolutionized the way businesses operate and consumers shop.

Advantages of E-Commerce

  • Convenience
  • Global Reach
  • Cost Savings
  • 24/7 Availability
  • Wide Variety of Choices

Disadvantages of E-Commerce

  • Security Concerns
  • Lack of Personal Interaction
  • Dependency on Technology
  • Legal and Regulatory Issues
  • Infrastructure and Connectivity Challenges

Security in E-Commerce

Security is a major concern in e-commerce. Measures such as encryption, digital signatures, and secure payment gateways are used to protect sensitive information and ensure safe transactions.

Digital Signature

A digital signature is a cryptographic technique used to authenticate the identity of a sender and ensure the integrity of a message or document. It involves the use of public and private keys to create and verify signatures.

Secure Electronic Transaction (SET)

SET is a protocol used for secure credit card transactions over the internet. It provides encryption and authentication mechanisms to protect sensitive payment information during online transactions.
